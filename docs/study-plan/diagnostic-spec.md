# Personalized Diagnostic — Canonical Spec

This is the single source of truth. It replaces `diagnostic-questions-draft.md`.
Four skills: **Reading · Listening · Speaking · Writing**. Each produces a score **band**.

## The one rule (applies everywhere)
> **The lowest band wins.** Inside a skill, the most basic mistake sets the ceiling — getting
> an advanced item right does not raise it. That same lowest broken thing is what we recommend
> studying first.

## What the student sees (the report)
- **Predicted score** (headline) = the **average** of the 4 skill numbers.
- **4 skill bars** = each skill's number (the **midpoint** of its band).
- **Fix this first** = the **lowest** skill, with the exact mistake quoted back.
- Any skill **below the student's desired score** → goes into the prioritized study plan.
  For free users the lessons/banks show **locked**.

# SKILL 1 — READING (2 parts)

Reading band = the **lower** of Part A and Part B. But the report explains **both**, because
they mean different things:
- **Part A (fill-in-blank)** = the **grammar + vocabulary foundation** for reading.
- **Part B (passage)** = the ability to **understand from context, without grammar**.

If Part B is high but Part A is low → "you can guess from context to read at a high level, but
grammar & vocabulary questions drag you down." → recommend **vocabulary-reading + fill-in-blank**
practice (mini-lesson + exam bank).

## Part A — Fill in the blank: "The Little Bakery"

> Anna owns a small bakery in the centre of town. Every morning, she **mix___ (1)** the flour and
> water, and then she **bak___ (2)** fresh bread for her customers. At the moment, she is
> **dec___ (3)** a beautiful cake for a special birthday order.
>
> Anna did not always own a shop. Ten years ago, she **stu___ (4)** cooking at a small college in
> France. After she finished her course, she **wor___ (5)** in a famous restaurant for two years.
> Then she **sav___ (6)** enough money and returned home to open her own bakery.
>
> Today, her artisan loaves are renowned throughout the region. Each cake is meticulously
> **cra___ (7)** by hand from locally sourced ingredients. Anna often reflects that, had she not
> taken such a risk, her business would never have **flo___ (8)**. She remains convinced that if her
> closely guarded recipes were ever **dis___ (9)**, the bakery's reputation would be irreparably
> damaged.

The word-stems are given on purpose — this tests grammar **form**, not vocabulary recall.

| Blank | Answer | Tier |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **mixes** | T1 present (-es after x) |
| 2 | **bakes** | T1 present (-s) |
| 3 | **decorating** | T1 present continuous |
| 4 | **studied** | T2 past (y→ied) |
| 5 | **worked** | T2 past (regular -ed) |
| 6 | **saved** | T2 past (drop e + -ed) |
| 7 | **crafted** | T3 present passive |
| 8 | **flourished** | T3 inverted 3rd conditional |
| 9 | **disclosed** | T3 passive inside conditional |

**Scoring (lowest wins):**
- Any error in blanks **1–3** → **below 80**
- Any error in blanks **4–6** → **below 110**
- Any error in blanks **7–9** → **below 120**
- All 9 correct → **120+**

Grading: case-insensitive exact match to the answer (+ obvious accepted variants).

## Part B — Passage: "Greenwashing in the UK"

> **P1** Greenwashing is when a company pretends to be more environmentally friendly than it really
> is. In the UK, this has become a common problem. Many businesses use words like "green,"
> "natural," or "eco-friendly" on their products because they know that shoppers care about the
> planet. However, these claims are not always true, and customers can easily be misled.
> **P2** ❓ MISSING
> **P3** To deal with this problem, the UK government has introduced new rules. The Competition and
> Markets Authority now checks that environmental claims are honest and clear. Companies that break
> the rules can be fined or forced to change their advertising. Experts also advise shoppers to look
> for real evidence, such as official labels, instead of trusting vague words on a package.

| Q | Question | Answer |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Which paragraph fills the gap? | **C** |
| 2 | Highlight the reason in P1 | **"because they know that shoppers care about the planet"** |
| 3 | Main idea | **B** (False environmental claims mislead UK shoppers, but action is being taken) |
| 4 | Best title | **D** (Greenwashing: Fake Green Claims in Britain) |

**Scoring (lowest wins):**
- **Q3 AND Q4 both wrong** → **below 100**
- **Any one of Q1 / Q3 / Q4 wrong** → **below 110**
- **Q2 (highlight) wrong** → **below 115**
- **All correct** → **above 130**

Q2 grading: the selection must contain the reason phrase (overlap is fine, doesn't need to be
character-exact).

# SKILL 2 — LISTENING (3 dictation sentences)

Student listens and **types** the sentence. We compare typed text to the target.
"Accuracy %" = share of words typed correctly (spelling counts; commas count where the sentence
has one). **Lowest band wins.**

| # | Sentence | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | *I really enjoyed my trip to Japan because we ate traditional food every single day.* | below **90%** → **below 80** |
| 2 | *After we spent the afternoon wandering the cobblestone streets, we sat at a small café to soak up the atmosphere.* | not **100%** → **80–100** |
| 3 | *Eager to escape the limits of the metropolis, I embarked on a journey to immerse myself in the wilderness.* | below **90%** → **110–130** |

- Sentence 1 ≥90%, Sentence 2 =100%, Sentence 3 ≥90% all clear → **130+**.
- Position inside a band by accuracy (e.g. sentence 3 at 90% → ~110, near 100% → ~130).

**Report narrative:** "Listening isn't only about vocabulary — it's about conjugating and fixing
grammar **while** listening. You have a strong foundation; the dictation mini-lesson + dictation
exam bank take you 80 → 110 → 130."

# SKILL 3 — SPEAKING (1 task)

**Prompt:** "Describe your favorite travel experience." 90 seconds.

**Transcription:** live browser transcription **+ Deepgram** — keep the **raw** transcript.
Do **NOT** transcribe with Gemini: it auto-corrects the student's grammar and hides the very
mistakes we grade. Gemini-lite is used **only** to (a) add punctuation to the raw transcript,
then (b) assess it.

**Scoring (lowest wins):**

| Condition | Band |
|---|---|
| Any **present tense** mistake | **below 80** |
| Any **past tense** mistake | **below 90** |
| Any **article** mistake | **below 95** |
| **Under 100 words** | **below 95** |
| Any **basic (B1) vocabulary** mistake | **below 95** |
| No grammar mistakes, uses **B2 vocabulary** | **110** |
| No grammar mistakes, **0–2** B1 collocations | **110** |
| No grammar mistakes, **4–5** B1 collocations, all correct | **120** |
| No grammar mistakes, **4–5** B1 collocations **+ ≥1 B2** collocation, all correct | **130** |

**Report:** state the score and the reason ("you fit this band because…"). If below desired:
recommend speaking mini-lessons + exam bank. If present/past mistakes → "grammar is weak for your
target." If under 100 words → "idea-development problem" → speaking mini-course + exam bank.

### Collocation rubric (for counting)
**B1:** catch a flight · book a hotel · pack a suitcase · take a tour · buy souvenirs ·
beautiful scenery · traditional food · historic building · direct flight · popular destination
**B2:** soak up the atmosphere · broaden your horizons · escape the crowds · sample the cuisine ·
wander the streets · breathtaking views · picturesque village · vibrant nightlife · rich heritage ·
remote location
**C1:** embark on a journey · quench your wanderlust · savor the delicacies · traverse the
landscape · forge memories · sweeping vistas · unspoiled wilderness · bustling metropolis ·
profound impact · intrepid traveler

# SKILL 4 — WRITING (2 multiple-choice exercises)

Writing band = the **lower** of Exercise 1 and Exercise 2.

**Tiers (lowest wins):**
- **Verb form / agreement OR preposition mistake** → **below 80** *(preposition counts with verb agreement)*
- Verb correct, but **article** mistake → **below 110**
- Everything correct **except punctuation** → **110–120**
- All correct → **120+**

### Exercise 1 — photo description
Answers & category: **1=B** article · **2=A** punctuation · **3=D** punctuation · **4=C** preposition →
verb tier · **5=B** punctuation · **6=C** verb agreement.

### Exercise 2 — essay
Answers & category: **1=B** verb agreement · **2=B** article · **3=B** punctuation · **4=B** verb
agreement · **5=B** punctuation · **6=B** article.

**Report:** if verb/preposition/article mistakes → "your foundation is too weak for your target
writing score" → mini-lesson + exam bank (shown **locked**). If only punctuation wrong → "your
foundation is good for 100–110, you just need a push for the high score."

# HOW THE NUMBERS COMBINE

1. Each skill → one band (lowest of its parts).
2. Convert each band to a representative number:

| Band | Number |
|---|---|
| below 80 | 75 |
| 80–90 / below 90 | 85 |
| below 95 / 80–100 | 90 |
| below 100 | 95 |
| below 110 / 100–110 | 105 |
| below 115 | 108 |
| below 120 / 110–120 | 115 |
| 110–130 | 120 |
| 120 | 120 |
| 130 / above 130 | 132 |

3. **Predicted score** = average of the 4 skill numbers.
4. **Bars** = the 4 skill numbers. **Fix-first** = the lowest skill.
5. Every skill below the **desired** score → enters the prioritized plan (locked for free users).
